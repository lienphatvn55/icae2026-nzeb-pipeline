3.5 Retrofit parameter space 
Discretization strategy for the retrofit decision space
Each retrofit parameter is discretized into a finite set of levels rather than treated as a continuous range. This choice is dictated by the downstream life-cycle requirements: every level must correspond to a commercially available product or a realizable operational setting so that an embodied-carbon factor (A1–A3), a service life (for B4 replacement), and a capital and replacement cost can be unambiguously assigned to it. Continuous ranges, by contrast, would generate intermediate values with no physical product behind them, for which neither LCA nor LCC quantities could be defined. The operational carbon term (B6) is not a level-bound attribute and is therefore excluded from this product-level inventory; it is derived separately from the simulated site energy use, the grid emission factor, and the analysis period, as defined in Section 3.6.
The number of levels is not fixed uniformly across parameters. Because the sampling is performed by Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS), which samples each dimension independently, parameters are not constrained to share a common level count, unlike screening designs that require a symmetric low–medium–high structure. The level count of each parameter is instead governed by the granularity of the real product market: equipment that is only available in discrete performance tiers (e.g., chiller COP) is represented by fewer levels, whereas near-continuous capacities (e.g., PV and battery sizing) admit a finer discretization at no additional data-collection cost. Across the parameter set, three to four levels per variable are adopted to balance design-space resolution and the life-cycle data that each additional level requires, consistent with discretization densities reported in comparable building-renovation optimization studies.
Each level range is anchored at the calibrated baseline and extended toward improved performance, so that the optimizer retains the "no-intervention" option alongside progressively deeper retrofit measures. Level directions follow the cooling-dominated nature of the Aw climate: glazing solar heat gain coefficient and lighting power density decrease from their baseline values, roof solar reflectance increases, and envelope U-values decrease.
